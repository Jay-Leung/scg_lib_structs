PIP-seq

The PIP-seq method can be used to perform high-throughput droplet single cell RNA-seq without microfluidic device (well ... you still need one to generate the hydrogel beads). Mix your cells, RT reagents, barcoded hydrogel beads and oil in a tube, then a simple vortex. You are done with droplets generation and cell encapsulation. It is for real!

The barcode design is based on the previous study published in Scientific Reports: Modular barcode beads for microfluidic single cell genomics. Delley2021.

NOTE: there are some differences between the paper version (published in Nature Biotechnology) and the commercial version (FluentBio). It does not matter that much in terms of sequencing, because the sequencing and read structure is the same in both versions.

NOTE2: The sequences used in this page are not consistent with the published paper or the commercial kit. I'm currently investigating the details and will update soon. For now, this page should give you a rough idea about how PIP-seq works in principle.
